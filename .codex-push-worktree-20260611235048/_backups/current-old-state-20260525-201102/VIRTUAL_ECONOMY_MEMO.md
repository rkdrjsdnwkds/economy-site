# Virtual Domestic Economy Memo

Trigger words: 가상내수경제, 내수경제, 가상경제

When the user says one of the trigger words, summarize this deferred feature idea:

- Split the future economy system into domestic economy and trade.
- Domestic economy uses a virtual country with virtual citizens.
- Citizens exist by income class ratio and each class has required goods plus willingness to pay.
- Virtual companies produce goods; each good needs about 2-3 materials.
- Material suppliers exist at different price and quality levels.
- Finished-good quality is derived from base product quality plus material quality tiers.
- Cost changes according to material quality and resulting product quality.
- Students set sale prices.
- Citizens buy by comparing quality and price against willingness to pay.
- If similar choices exist, promotion/marketing influences purchase selection.
- Student roles can include material producer, product company, and marketing company.
- Students do not start with full company creation.
- Early stage: students make materials and supply them to virtual product companies.
- Each virtual company has a daily procurement budget/quota; once filled, it stops accepting deliveries.
- After a student can produce at least 5 units per day, they can create a company.
- With enough cash, students can pay a high registration fee to start a finished-goods company.
- Marketing companies are chosen by student vote.
- Future expansion may add primary industries such as agriculture and mining.

Do not implement this all at once. Treat it as a later phased update idea.
